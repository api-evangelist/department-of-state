<p><b><i>There was no change to the advisory level or risk indicators. Advisory summary was updated.</i></b></p>
<p>Exercise increased caution in <b>Bolivia </b>due to <b>crime and civil unrest</b>. Some areas have increased risk. Read the entire Travel Advisory.</p>
<p><b>Do Not Travel</b> to <b>Chapare Province</b> due to <b>crime</b>.</p>
<p><b>Advisory Summary</b></p>
<ul>
<li>U.S. government employees working in Bolivia need special authorization to travel to Chapare Province due to a higher incidence of crimes associated with narcotrafficking.</li>
<li>Petty crime is common, especially in popular tourist spots. Stay aware of your surroundings.</li>
<li>U.S. government employees working in Bolivia are encouraged to pay extra attention to their surroundings in El Alto because of petty crime.</li>
<li>Large demonstrations about local politics and the economic situation can happen with little warning. Demonstrations may block transportation and other essential services.</li>
<li>Medical services for routine and emergency procedures are limited in rural areas. Review the <a><u></u></a><u><a href="#health" title="Health Travel Guidance">Health section of the Travel Guidance</a></u>&nbsp;for more information.</li>
<li>Consider the risk of <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travel-to-high-altitudes" target="_blank" title="CDC Traveling to High Altitude Locations">traveling to high altitude locations</a> in Bolivia.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Level: 4 &#8211; Do not travel</b></p>
<p><b>Chapare Province<br />
Do not travel</b> to <b>Chapare Province</b> due to<b> crime.</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Do not travel to Chapare Province for any reason.</li>
<li>Significant amounts of coca are produced illegally in Chapare province.&nbsp; Local police presence is limited and there is a higher prevalence of violent crime associated with narcotrafficking.</li>
<li>U.S. government employees working in Bolivia need special authorization to travel to Chapare Province.</li>
<li>The U.S. government has limited ability to offer emergency services to U.S. citizens in Chapare Province.</li>
<li>Review our information on <a href="https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/travel-advisories/high-risk-areas.html?" title="Travel to High-Risk Areas">Travel to High-Risk Areas</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>If you decide to travel to Bolivia:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Enroll in the <a href="https://mytravel.state.gov/s/step" title="Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP)">Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP)</a> to get important updates and alerts from the U.S. embassy or consulate.&nbsp; Enrolling helps the U.S. embassy or consulate contact you or your emergency contact in an emergency.</li>
<li>Review the <a href="https://www.osac.gov/Country/Bolivia/Content/Detail/Report/cfbd21df-ae93-4df2-8e89-1c87f70c57f5" title="Country Security Report">Country Security Report</a>.</li>
<li>Review our information on <a href="https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/travel-advisories/crime.html" title="Crime Abroad">Crime Abroad</a> and for <a href="https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/help-abroad/victims-crime.html" title="Victims of Crime">Victims of Crime</a>.</li>
<li>We highly recommend that you buy travel insurance before you travel. Check with your <a href="https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/planning/guidance/insurance.html" title="Travel Inusrance">travel insurance provider</a> about evacuation assistance, medical insurance, and trip cancelation coverage.</li>
<li>Do not physically resist any robbery attempt.</li>
<li>Stay aware of your surroundings.</li>
<li>Stay alert in tourist spots.</li>
<li>Carry a copy of your passport and visa (if applicable). &nbsp;Leave originals in your hotel safe.</li>
<li>Have a plan to leave in an emergency that does not depend on U.S. government help. Review our information on <a href="https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/help-abroad/crisis-response.html" title="Crisis and Evacuations">Crisis and Evacuations</a>.</li>
<li>Avoid demonstrations and crowds.</li>
<li>Check local media for breaking news. &nbsp;Be prepared to adjust your plans.</li>
<li>Refer to our list of <a href="https://bo.usembassy.gov/medical-assistance/" title="Bolivia U.S. Embassy list of Medical Providers">medical providers</a>.</li>
<li>Review the CDC&#8217;s page on <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travel-to-high-altitudes" target="_blank" title="CDC Travel to High Altitudes">Travel to High Altitudes</a>.</li>
<li>Make sure your insurance includes medical evacuation coverage.</li>
<li>Check with your doctor about required vaccines and shots for high-risk exposure before you go to Bolivia. &nbsp;Review the Health section in the Travel Guidance for Bolivia.</li>
<li>Visit the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) website for the <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/destinations/traveler/none/bolivia?s_cid=ncezid-dgmq-travel-single-001" target="_blank" title="CDC Travel Health Information">latest Travel Health Information</a> for Bolivia.</li>
<li>Whether you&#8217;re a first time or frequent traveler, use the <a href="https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/planning/checklist.html" title="International Travel Checklist">International Travel Checklist</a>.</li>
</ul>
